# Brand Context: The Ritual Journal Collective

## Site Identity
- Name: The Ritual Journal Collective
- Domain: theritualjournalcollective.com
- Framing: Independent editorial publication (NOT a personal blog)
- Aesthetic: Psychology Today / editorial wellness publication. Light cream background, charcoal text, serif headings, warm earth tones (stone, sand, rust). NO green.

## Mission
Cover the intersection of ancient ritual practice and modern nervous system science — specifically polyvagal theory, IFS, and somatic healing as applied to grief, loss, and emotional processing.

## Voice and Style Rules
- Short sentences. No fluff.
- Always answer the target query in the first 40-60 words. No "In this article we will..." preamble.
- All H2s are questions or clear topic statements
- "I" voice in "My experience" sections only
- Never mention credentials (PhD, LCSW, etc.) — use experience-based language
- Cite real studies from PubMed/NIH/APA — use actual DOIs when possible
- No em dashes (—). Use colons or commas instead.
- Year in all content: 2026

## Do Not
- Mention "How Minds Work" by name (say "the How Minds Work channel" in product recs only)
- Use green color anywhere
- Link to howmindswork.org from the main nav or sitewide elements
- Claim medical credentials for any author
- Write "In this article" or "Let's explore" or "Dive into"
