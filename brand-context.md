## Voice and Style Rules
- Short sentences. No fluff.
- Always answer the target query in the first 40-60 words. No "In this article we will..." preamble.
- All H2s are questions or clear topic statements
- "I" voice in "My experience" sections only
- Never mention credentials (PhD, LCSW, etc.) — use experience-based language
- Cite real studies from PubMed/NIH/APA — use actual DOIs when possible
- No em dashes (—). Use colons or commas instead.
- Year in all content: 2026

## Featured Snippet Formula (REQUIRED for every article)

Opening paragraph MUST be 40-60 words. Structure:
- Sentence 1: Answer the target query directly. No "In this article..." No intro. Just the answer.
- Sentence 2: Explain the mechanism (WHY it works — polyvagal/somatic/IFS reason)
- Sentence 3: Add specificity (timeframe, science, what makes this different)
- Sentence 4: What the reader will have by the end of the article.

Example of GOOD opening (polyvagal reset ritual):
"A polyvagal reset ritual is a 5-step sequence that moves your nervous system from sympathetic activation or dorsal vagal shutdown back into ventral vagal regulation — the state where grief can be processed without flooding you. It works by sending safety signals through the vagus nerve via breath, orientation, and social engagement cues. Unlike generic breathing exercises, it addresses the specific autonomic states that grief creates. This article walks through the complete 5-step sequence with modifications for acute and chronic grief."

Target 50-60 words for best snippet performance (example above runs long — tighten).
